Architect

I call myself a ‘Software Architect’. What does that mean? Well, for me, it means that every piece of software I write gets designed first. I use my experience to decide on what overall structure meets the requirements, and then pick what tools I’m going to use to build that structure. Every decision I make while writing code is thought out, and I attempt to look ahead to where this project is likely to go, and make sure my design will accommodate that.

I do this universally, usually in my head, or on a whiteboard. From code comments to project layout, to the libraries I use, everything is a conscious, reasoned decision. That doesn't mean I always get it right, mind you. Reevaluation is part of learning and improving, and what wasn't well suited 6 months ago might be a perfect fit now. Everything's a balancing act, and I've gotten pretty good at it.

Adaptable

I believe that the single most important life skill is learning how to change your mind. It’s an absolutely essential life skill, and sadly, one of the largest causes for contention and misguided decisions. So much pain, strife and horror has happened in this world simply because people didn’t know how to step back, accept that they were wrong and that’s ok.

I pride myself on the fact that I am aware of my own stubbornness, and am actively improving my abilities in this area. I'm not perfect, nor am I even where I want to be, but I strive to improve in this area on a daily basis. Additionally, I am working to improve myself as a rationalist.
